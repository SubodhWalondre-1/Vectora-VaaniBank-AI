# Home Loan — Union Bank of India

## Eligibility
- Minimum CIBIL score: 720 for loan amount above 30 lakh; 700 for below 30 lakh
- Age: 21 to 70 years at loan maturity
- Salaried: Minimum 2 years of employment, stable income
- Self-employed: Minimum 3 years of business continuity, ITR for 2 years
- Minimum monthly income: Rs. 25,000 for salaried; Rs. 30,000 net profit for self-employed

## Loan Parameters
- Minimum loan amount: Rs. 5 lakh
- Maximum loan amount: Rs. 10 crore (subject to property value and income)
- LTV ratio: Up to 90% (for loans <= 30L); 80% (30L to 75L); 75% (> 75L)
- Maximum tenure: 30 years
- Interest rate: 7.15% to 9.60% p.a. (floating, linked to EBLR)
- Concession: 0.05% for female sole/primary owners
- Processing fee: 0.50% of loan amount (max Rs. 15,000 + GST)

## EMI Reference (approximate at 8.75% per annum)
- 20 lakh for 20 years: approx Rs. 17,700/month
- 30 lakh for 20 years: approx Rs. 26,500/month
- 40 lakh for 20 years: approx Rs. 35,400/month
- 50 lakh for 20 years: approx Rs. 44,200/month
- 50 lakh for 30 years: approx Rs. 39,300/month

## Required Documents — Salaried
1. Aadhaar card (original + self-attested copy)
2. PAN card (mandatory, RBI rule)
3. Last 3 months salary slips
4. Last 6 months bank statement (salary account)
5. Form 16 for last 2 years
6. Property documents: sale deed, NOC from builder/society
7. Property valuation report (from bank-approved valuer)
8. Passport size photograph (2 copies)
9. Employment proof: offer letter or company ID card

## Required Documents — Self Employed
1. Aadhaar card
2. PAN card (mandatory)
3. ITR for last 2 years with computation
4. CA-certified profit and loss statement
5. Business registration certificate
6. Last 12 months bank statement (current account)
7. Property documents: same as salaried

## Step-by-Step Process
Step 1: Verify CIBIL score — if below 720, advise customer to improve before applying
Step 2: Collect income proof and check eligibility (max loan = approx 60x monthly income)
Step 3: Property shortlisting — confirm LTV does not exceed 80%
Step 4: Submit application form with KYC documents
Step 5: Bank legal and technical verification of property (7-10 working days)
Step 6: Sanction letter issued after approval
Step 7: Loan disbursement after property registration

## Key Rules
- PAN card mandatory for all loan transactions above Rs. 50,000 (RBI circular)
- Joint applicant (co-borrower) increases eligible loan amount
- Prepayment allowed with no penalty for floating rate loans
- Insurance: PMAY subsidy available for first-time buyers under EWS/LIG/MIG category
