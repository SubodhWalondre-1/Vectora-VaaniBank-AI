# Loan Application — Step-by-Step Process

## Overview
Process for home loan, personal loan, vehicle loan, or education loan application.
Estimated time from application to sanction: 7-15 working days.

Step 1: Verify CIBIL score
Ask customer if they know their CIBIL score.
Minimum required: 720 for home loan above 30 lakh; 700 for below 30 lakh.
For personal loan: minimum 700.
If CIBIL below minimum — advise customer to clear dues and apply after 6 months.
CIBIL score check is free once per year at cibil.com.

Step 2: Confirm eligibility based on income
Home loan: maximum loan amount = approximately 60 times net monthly income.
Personal loan: maximum Rs. 15 lakh; income must be at least Rs. 15,000/month.
Existing EMIs reduce eligibility — total EMI burden should not exceed 50% of income.

Step 3: Collect KYC documents
Aadhaar card (original + self-attested copy).
PAN card (mandatory under RBI circular — no exceptions).
Proof of income: salary slips (last 3 months) or ITR (last 2 years for self-employed).
Bank statement: last 6 months from salary/primary account.

Step 4: Property documents (home loan only)
Sale deed or agreement to sell.
NOC from builder or housing society.
Property valuation report from bank-approved valuer.
Approved building plan from municipal authority.

Step 5: Submit loan application form
Fill CBS application form with customer details.
Customer signs form — joint applicant signs if applicable.
Collect processing fee: 0.50% of loan amount (minimum Rs. 10,000 for home loan).

Step 6: Bank verification process
Legal verification of property documents — 3-5 working days.
Technical verification (site visit by bank valuer) — 2-3 working days.
Credit appraisal by loan officer — 2-3 working days.

Step 7: Sanction letter and disbursement
Sanction letter issued after approval — valid for 6 months.
Customer accepts sanction terms.
Disbursement after property registration and mortgage creation.
For under-construction property: stage-wise disbursement linked to construction progress.

## Key Rules
- PAN mandatory for all loan applications — no exceptions under RBI rules
- Co-borrower (spouse, parent) increases eligibility and reduces interest rate in some schemes
- Prepayment penalty: nil for floating rate loans; may apply for fixed rate
- PMAY subsidy: first-time buyers under EWS (income up to Rs. 3 lakh), LIG (Rs. 3-6 lakh), MIG (Rs. 6-18 lakh) eligible for interest subsidy of 3-6.5%
