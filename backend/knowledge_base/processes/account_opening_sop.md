# Account Opening — Step-by-Step Process

## Overview
New savings or current account opening at Union Bank of India branch counter.
Estimated time: 20-30 minutes if all documents are in order.

Step 1: Greet customer and confirm type of account needed
Confirm whether customer wants savings, current, BSBD (Jan Dhan), or salary account.
Ask if they are a new customer or existing account holder.

Step 2: Check PMJDY eligibility
If customer has no bank account anywhere, they are eligible for PMJDY (Jan Dhan) scheme.
PMJDY benefits: zero minimum balance, RuPay debit card, Rs. 2 lakh accident insurance, overdraft up to Rs. 10,000 after 6 months.

Step 3: Collect and verify KYC documents
Aadhaar card — verify name, address, and date of birth.
PAN card — mandatory for accounts with transactions above Rs. 50,000.
Passport-size photographs — minimum 2 copies.
Address proof if Aadhaar address is different from current address.

Step 4: Fill account opening form
Staff enters customer details in CBS (Core Banking System).
Customer signs form — verify signature matches ID proof.
Collect initial deposit amount (minimum Rs. 1000 for urban savings, Rs. 500 for rural, Rs. 0 for BSBD).

Step 5: Issue temporary passbook and debit card request
Give customer a temporary passbook or account number slip.
Submit debit card request — card delivered within 7-10 working days.
Activate internet banking if customer requests — provide UNI Mobile app instructions.

Step 6: Aadhaar seeding and mobile linking
Link Aadhaar to account for DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) benefits.
Link mobile number for OTP and SMS alerts.
Confirm nomination — nominee name, relation, date of birth.

Step 7: Account activation confirmation
Account is active immediately after form submission.
First transaction requires in-person visit.
Inform customer about minimum balance, penalty charges if applicable.


## Key Rules
- Aadhaar is mandatory for all new accounts under PMLA guidelines
- PAN or Form 60 (declaration) required if PAN not available
- Joint accounts allowed — all holders must complete KYC
- Minor accounts: parent/guardian KYC required, converted at age 18
